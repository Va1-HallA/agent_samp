Hypertensive emergency definition: systolic >=180 mmHg or diastolic >=110 mmHg, with or without target-organ damage.

Management protocol:
1. Notify the on-duty physician immediately. Do not self-escalate antihypertensive dosage (avoid abrupt drop causing cerebral hypoperfusion).
2. Place the patient in semi-Fowler position, maintain airway, administer oxygen at 2-3 L/min.
3. Monitor blood pressure every 15 minutes until below safe range (under 160/100).
4. Record symptoms: headache, blurred vision, chest pain, nausea. Escalate immediately if any symptom appears.
5. Notify emergency contact and prepare transfer materials.

Common medications: nifedipine sustained-release (do not split controlled-release), captopril (sublingual 12.5 mg in emergencies).
Contraindications: vigorous activity, emotional agitation, hot showers.
